UX and Agile are not natural enemies. Let’s stop treating them that way.

The biggest irony of the shift to Agile is that it’s exactly what the UX world has been seeking for years. Yet, now that it’s here, it seems we’re wholly unprepared for it. The tables have turned and developers are ready to iterate quickly. However, our standard UX toolbox feels inadequate for the task.

Our challenge is to become effective contributors in this Agile world. We need to start adapting to it.

We have to cut our designs into sprint-sized chunks while ensuring that it feels like a coherent, well-thought-out design when we put the pieces together.. Unfortunately, conventional thinking about Agile doesn’t help us with this.

Agile was not conceived with UX in mind — we’re on our own to make this work. Now is the time to tame the chaos and take control of Agile UX.
