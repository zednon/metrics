UX Metrics make the invisible visible.

Effective UX metrics track the critical improvements to the user experience of your products or services.

With robust metrics, your UX team’s work and the benefits that come from that work become highly visible. UX metrics make your work visible to your stakeholders, to your executives, and across your organization.

Not all UX metrics are equal.

We hear about UX metrics often. They sometimes have fancy names, such as HEART, SUS, Net Promoter Score, time on page, conversion rate, task time, satisfaction, or sentiment analysis.

These conventional metrics are what teams gravitate to. Unfortunately, these metrics do not make their work more visible.

Conventional metrics embody magical thinking. These metrics don’t help your organization see the value of UX. They don’t make your UX effort more visible.

They often end up hiding the real achievements the team has had because these conventional. What good is it doing if your metrics do not show improvements in the users’ experiences? Sometimes, it’s not clear what they show. (We’re looking at you, Net Promoter Score.)

Are you measuring the UX improvements you’re delivering?

Conventional UX metrics don’t measure a user’s actual experience. They don’t measure what was frustrating about the experience for the user. They don’t show when new designs have eliminated those frustrations.

Conventional UX metrics don’t measure when your users and customers find your unique designs delightful. They don’t measure when you’ve exceeded the users’ expectations or anticipated your customers’ needs.

Conventional UX metrics don’t measure whether your team has improved users’ or customers’ lives. What good is all your hard work if your UX isn’t improving your users’ lives?

If you don’t know if your customers’ and users’ lives improved because of your UX work, how will your stakeholders and executives? You need far better metrics to make these improvements work.

Directly connect your organization to their users.

Outcome-driven UX Metrics

Measuring your users’ actual experience.

Outcome-driven UX Metrics is our radical approach to combating the issues caused by conventional metrics.  

When you use Outcome-driven UX Metrics, you’ll directly track the user experience improvements your team makes.  

It’s not based on the magical thinking you find in conventional UX metrics approaches. No more complicated explanations about how conversion rates, NPS, or the HEART framework demonstrate how the experience is better for customers and users. No more explaining why numbers didn’t change, even though you improved your design. No more guessing why your numbers change by themselves, even though you didn’t deliver anything new.

Outcome-driven UX Metrics focus you, your team, stakeholders, and executives on improving your customers’ and users’ lives. These improvements are so exceptional that customers immediately see the enhanced value, and users see increased benefits.

Outcome-driven UX Metrics make UX distinctively visible. 

Showcase precisely where you’re delivering value

You’ll start with what we call a UX Outcome. Your UX outcome answers a simple question: How will you improve your users’ and customers’ lives? Ultimately, you’ll build your metrics around this question.

Using your UX outcome, you’ll create three categories of metrics:

  • UX Success metrics: Report the precise moment you’ve improved your customers’ and users' lives.
  • UX Progress metrics: Achieve key results as your team progresses against your planned milestones.
  • Problem-value metrics: Pinpoint and aggregate your organization’s ongoing costs of poor UX to promote UX’s return on investment.

Each of these metrics categories helps you tell the story of your UX journey. The story of where you’re going, where you started, and how far along you are.

Outcome-driven UX Metrics are simple in their concepts. Once you acquire the fundamentals, you’re ready to implement your own.

And once you implement your own Outcome-Driven UX Metrics, you’ve unleashed a superpower. You’ll spark exceptional outcomes with your UX metrics as the foundation.
